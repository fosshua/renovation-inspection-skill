# Severity and Confidence

## Severity Levels

`critical`
: Immediate or potentially severe risk to personal safety, structural safety, fire safety, gas safety, major electrical safety, or major waterproofing failure. Recommend stopping related work and engaging a qualified professional or responsible contractor.

`high`
: Clear standards or safety concern, high rework cost, likely functional failure, concealed-work risk before closure, or issue that can materially affect acceptance.

`medium`
: Visible quality, sequencing, installation, or workmanship issue with moderate functional or rework impact.

`low`
: Minor workmanship or finish issue that is visible and should be corrected but is unlikely to create major functional risk.

`info`
: Observation, evidence gap, maintenance note, or item requiring measurement before judgment.

## Ranking Criteria

Rank by:
1. Safety risk.
2. Standards or acceptance risk.
3. Likelihood of hidden follow-on damage.
4. Rework cost if delayed.
5. Urgency before the next construction stage.
6. User-stated concern.

## Confidence Levels

`high`
: Evidence is clear, issue is visible, and a relevant source basis applies.

`medium`
: Evidence supports the concern, but measurement, angle, lighting, source specificity, or project context is incomplete.

`low`
: Concern is plausible but relies mainly on visual judgment, partial views, uncertain scale, or missing context.

## Hidden-Work Guardrail

If a concealed system is not visible, use language such as:

"当前证据不能确认隐蔽层做法是否合格；需要补充隐蔽验收照片、试验记录或现场复核。"

Do not write:

"隐蔽工程不合格"

unless the provided evidence directly shows the nonconforming concealed condition.
